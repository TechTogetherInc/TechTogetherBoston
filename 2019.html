Our Mission

At TechTogether Boston, we're looking to shape the way women get involved with tech.

We aim create an inclusive environment to encourage more underrepresented people to either get introduced to the world of technology or harness their skills to create projects of their own.

With the support of the technology community, industry, and individuals we can chip away at the gender gap and empower anyone from someone with a non-technical major with an interest in tech to seasoned hackathon veterans looking to build out a complicated, groundbreaking product with a little extra support.

What is a hackathon?


You'll enjoy a weekend of awesome tech, food, swag, workshops, and mini-events while learning from distinguished speakers, inspiring mentors, and peers.

2018 Hacker Highlights

Looking back on some of our favorite projects from SheHacks 2018

stars

 SOCIAL IMPACT WINNER

Ladies in Technology (LIT)

Women empowering app to help grow your network, share ideas, and make new friends!

Built with: Java, Android Studio, Firebase, HTML/CSS, Kotlin

stars

 BEST BEGINNER HACK

PuppyTime

Platform to help shelters with donations and adoption by connecting people with a live stream of adorable shelter pups! In addition to a live stream, viewers are also able to remotely give them treats.

Built with: HTML/ CSS, jQuery, Bootstrap, AWS, C, Arduino board

stars

 Funniest Hack Winner

Extensions Extension

Chrome extension that gives you extensions! Limited to facial hair and wigs. Instantly grow a fabulous mustache at the click of button.

Built with: JavaScript, HTML/CSS, jQuery, JSON, Azure, C

stars

 Best VR Hack Winner

Final Meltdown

This VR browser experience simulates icebergs melting all around you as a way to see the negative effects of climate change. Includes polar bears that are also looking for solid ice to stand on.

Built with: A-frame, Glitch, JavaScript, Three.js
